I am a machine learning researcher working on intelligent human–AI interaction, with expertise in wearable sensing, computer vision, multimodal learning, and adaptive intelligent systems. My doctoral research at Memorial University of Newfoundland investigates AI-driven prosthetic hand control using wearable sensing and computer vision, with a focus on adaptive machine learning and personalized control. Previously, my M.Sc. research at Amirkabir University of Technology focused on computer vision, affective computing, and human behavior analysis, where I developed deep learning models for automated lecture quality assessment from video data. I am currently a Ph.D. Candidate in Computer Science at Memorial University of Newfoundland.
